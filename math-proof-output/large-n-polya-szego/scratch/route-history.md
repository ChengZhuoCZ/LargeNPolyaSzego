## Blind portfolio pilot: global localization family

On 2026-07-21 three information-isolated scouts independently returned the
same mechanism family: normalize to area $\pi$, use an inscribed-disk estimate
for $R_N(\pi)$ and quantitative Faber--Krahn stability to force every possible
challenger into Fraenkel distance $O(N^{-1})$ from the disk, then invoke a
ratio-free completed-action comparison.

The common exact artifact is
\[
 r_N^2=\frac{\pi}{N\tan(\pi/N)},\qquad
 0\le\lambda_1(R_N(\pi))-\lambda_1(B)
 \le \lambda_1(B)\left(\frac{N\tan(\pi/N)}\pi-1\right)
 =O(N^{-2}).
\]
Conditional on a correctly verified quantitative Faber--Krahn constant this
yields an $O(N^{-1})$ Fraenkel entry estimate. It does not control the smallest
fan angle or side ratio, and its residual local lemma is exactly `JC_PL`. The
pilot therefore records useful localization evidence but no strict reduction
of the current core and no cross-Family route diversity; it is not eligible
for route selection.

### Quantitative-localization refinement

Primary-text verification of Brasco--De Philippis--Velichkov, Main Theorem
(1.9), upgrades the pilot's global consequence. Together with the exact
regular-polygon inradius it proves that every hypothetical strict competitor
satisfies
\[
\mathcal A(P)\le C_{\mathrm{FK}}/N.
\]
This is a strict reduction of the global theorem to a proper near-disk class,
but it still does not reduce JC_PL or control microscopic fan ratios. The
family is therefore retained only as an incompatible global reserve.

## Blind cross-family kill certificates

Three information-isolated probes tested one-step bypasses with different
mathematical engines.

- Exact-\(N\) Steiner descent is killed by the parabola polygon
  \(v_j=(2^j,4^j)\): every Steiner axis produces at least \(2N-4>N\)
  sides for \(N\ge5\).
- Conformal weighted-Green Loewner domination is killed because localized
  quadratic-form order gives pointwise order of equal-integral conformal
  weights, hence equality and congruence.
- A radial cellwise semidefinite pullback is killed by
  \(\det C_i=1\) and \(N^{-1}\sum_iJ_i=1\): the proposed LMIs force every
  cell map to be the same Euclidean rotation.

The portable derivations are retained under scratch/crux-dossiers. None is a
counterexample to the frozen theorem; each refutes only its proposed route.

## Stage 4 QRCI interface split

The independent committed-route repair probe tested alternating, localized
balanced, and highest-frequency completed rays. All three have the required
natural cubic scale and give no fan-row counterexample. They also show why the
old compound JC-SP row cannot be audited as written: before a uniform
estimate, one must prove a common \(C^3\) material identity whose disjoint
full-cell ledger recombines terminal/complement and all endpoint descendants.

The old compound row is therefore superseded by JC-SP-QRCI (exact identity
and regularity) followed by JC-SP-EST (uniform natural-energy bound). This is
an interface correction inside the selected completed-action family, not a
route reset.

## Stage 4 boundary-regularity interface split

A second independent committed-route repair probe found no counterexample to
form-level \(C^3\) smoothness on a fixed material triangulation. It did show
that this form statement does not justify differentiating the actual
reduced-DtN, conormal trace, corner coefficient, and endpoint distribution
data three times in a named topology.

The compound JC-SP-QRCI row is therefore superseded by JC-SP-REG (boundary,
corner, trace, and reduced-resolvent regularity) followed by JC-SP-LEDGER (the
finite atom-indexed ownership identity). JC-SP-EST remains the later uniform
estimate. This is a second local interface correction, not a route reset.

## Stage 5 typed-trace interface replacement

The first proof-strategy type check of JC-SP-REG found that the full product of
edgewise \(H^{1/2}\) spaces is larger than the trace of one global \(H^1\)
function, so its claimed lifting did not exist.  It also found that the form
operators were applied to a lift outside their declared trial domain and that
one polygon family did not define the two advertised common/jump channels.

Attack A8 records this exact interface mismatch.  JC-SP-REG is stale and is
replaced by JC-SP-MATREG, which uses the compatible quotient trace spaces,
extended trial-slot forms, and both \(Y_t\) and \(B_t\) polygon families.
Independent continuation reviewer J returned continue-attack.  The selected
completed-action route and all quantitative obligations are unchanged.

## Stage 4 scalar/flux endpoint replacement

The independent counterexample scout found that the first typed replacement
still conflated scalar conormal pullback with weak flux-density pullback.  In a
fixed side coordinate their leading coefficients differ by one boundary
Jacobian, and the two incidences at a vertex can have different length ratios.

Attack A9 records the failure.  JC-SP-MATREG is stale and is replaced by
JC-SP-MATFLUX, whose endpoint block is indexed by vertex, incidence, and
common/jump channel.  This remains a local interface repair inside R-JC.
Independent continuation reviewer K returned continue-attack.

## Stage 5 dual-sector proof repair

The first dual audit accepted the fixed-domain form, eigenpair, reduced-DtN,
trace-space, and scalar/flux calculations but rejected the moving-sector
regularity bridge.  A generic \(H^1\) function cannot be differentiated after
composition with a moving seam, and the first proof did not sum differentiated
all-mode Bessel ratios in \(H^{1/2}\).

Attack A10 keeps JC-SP-MATFLUX whole and replaces only O5--O6 by a second
sector-compatible material-map IFT and an exponentially summable factored
Bessel-ratio estimate.
