# Crux Dossier

Family ID: F-QRCI-BOUNDARY-REG

## Core Obligation

Core bottleneck: justify the differentiated corner, reduced-DtN, trace, and
endpoint data before assembling the exact QRCI ownership ledger.

The superseded JC-SP-QRCI row combined two mechanisms: fixed-domain
form-level smoothness of the simple first eigenpair and third differentiation
of boundary/corner data used by the full-cell formula.

## D2 Derivation

On a closed active completed ray, a vertex-fixing piecewise-affine material map
pulls all polygons back to one \(H_0^1\) space. Its stiffness and mass
coefficients are \(C^3\) in support time as long as the finitely many triangle
Jacobians remain nonzero. Simplicity of the first Dirichlet eigenvalue then
supports a \(C^3\) normalized eigenpair at the form level.

That statement does not by itself imply \(C^3\) dependence of:

- the reduced resolvent and reduced-DtN blocks in their operator topologies;
- the conormal traces and endpoint pairings on moving polygonal sides;
- the coefficients of the corner powers and their logarithmic material
  descendants; or
- the endpoint distributions that cancel against annular complements.

The first replacement child JC-SP-REG must name the fixed weighted trace and
corner-data spaces and prove these \(C^3\) maps. Only then can the residual
JC-SP-LEDGER define a finite atom set and ownership map and prove
\[
D^3\mathbf A_N(tq)[q,q,q]
=\sum_{\xi\in\mathscr I_N}\mathfrak q_\xi(t;q)
\]
with every scalar descendant owned exactly once. JC-SP-EST remains the later
uniform estimate of that sum.

## Reduction Witness

Reduction witness: compound QRCI regularity-and-ledger => JC_SP_REG boundary regularity then JC_SP_LEDGER ownership identity; axis=regularity

The two claims have different failure modes: loss of weighted trace
differentiability versus omission or duplication in a finite algebraic
partition.

## Falsification

Alternating, localized, highest-frequency, near-side-collapse, and
corner-resonance families do not refute form-level \(C^3\). Exact wedge
calibration shows that the third exponent derivative produces at worst
square-integrable \(\log^3 r\) factors when the convex exponent approaches
\(1\). The unresolved issue is not integrability of that model term but
legitimate \(C^3\) dependence of the actual spectral boundary data in a named
space.

## Verdict

Verdict: split. Audit JC-SP-REG first, then assemble JC-SP-LEDGER, and only
afterward attempt JC-SP-EST.
