# F-RL-HARDY-CAYLEY: integrated Hardy reduction and pointwise obstruction

## Target

Prove `JC_CL_RL`, equivalently the strict endpoint comparison for the Burgers
entropy interpolation generated by a compactly supported zero-mean displacement
with distributional slope at least `-1`.

## Exact reduction

For the analytic signal (z_t=h_t+i\mathcal Hh_t), set

\[
p_t=t+t(1-t)z_t',\qquad
m(t)=\frac{\int_{\mathbb H}|z_t|^2\Re p_t}
{\int_{\mathbb H}|z_t|^2}.
\]

The slope bounds imply (0\le\Re p_t\le1), while the Hardy-area identity and
Burgers dissipation give

\[
\frac d{dt}\log E(h_t)=\frac{m(t)-t}{t(1-t)}.
\]

In the Minty coordinate (k), the endpoint transforms split as (C+D) and
(C-D), where

\[
C(\omega)=\omega^{-1}\int e^{-i\omega r}\sin(\omega k(r))\,dr,
\]

\[
D(\omega)=-i\omega^{-1}\int e^{-i\omega r}
\{1-\cos(\omega k(r))\}\,dr.
\]

Writing (S=\|C\|^2+\|D\|^2) and
(R=\Re\langle C,D\rangle) in
(L^2((0,\infty),d\omega/\omega)), the endpoint energies are
(E_\pm=S\pm2R). Hence `JC_CL_RL` is reduced to an absolute strict angle
bound

\[
|R|\le (1/6-\delta)S
\]

for every compactly supported mean-zero (1)-Lipschitz (k).

## Failed inference

The proposed pointwise estimate (m(t)\ge t/(2-t)) is false. For the exact
interval-collapse flow, differentiating the closed energy formula yields

\[
m(0.9)=0.7948739137\ldots<9/11.
\]

Thus the strip condition (0\le\Re p_t\le1) cannot be used one time at a
time. A fixed-period equality-exclusion argument also fails to cover coherent
long-period trains, whose real-line energies converge to the periodic endpoint
ratio through the Fejér comb.

## Decision

- Failure type: constant-estimate insufficient.
- Invalidated inference: a pointwise-in-time Carathéodory bound integrates to
  the endpoint factor.
- Strict reduction: the full real-line inverse-displacement inequality is
  equivalent to the single Cayley cross-term angle estimate above.
- Next decision test: prove the angle estimate uniformly under concentration,
  dichotomy, and coherent periodic trains, or construct a certified sequence
  with (|R|/S\to1/6).
