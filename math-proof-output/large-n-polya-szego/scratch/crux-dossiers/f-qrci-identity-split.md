## Falsification

The independent committed-route repair probe tested three exact scale
families:

- alternating fan data have no cubic fan obstruction;
- a localized balanced fan ray saturates the required \(a^{11/2}\) scale; and
- a highest-frequency support ray normalized by
  \(\mathcal G^{\mathrm{JP}}_{\alpha_*}(w)=\rho^2a^5\) also requires an
  \(O(a^{11/2})\) bound.

These checks produce no counterexample. They instead show that an uncancelled
inverse-angle or endpoint logarithm in an unrecombined expression would be
fatal. The scalar radial \(H^{1/2}\) divergence still kills only that shortcut.
