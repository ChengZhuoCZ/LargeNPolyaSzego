# Crux Dossier

Family ID: F-QRCI-DUAL-SECTOR

## Core Obligation

Repair the parameter-dependent corner extraction and strong conormal remainder
proof without composing an arbitrary \(H^1\) function with a moving physical
sector map.

## Exact Audit Failure

Two independent Stage 5 auditors agreed that the first O5--O6 draft did not
close.  A smooth moving diffeomorphism need not act differentiably on an
arbitrary \(H^1\) function; a translated derivative jump is an explicit
counterexample.  The draft also did not state the analytic finite-dimensional
dependence of \(h_\alpha,J_\alpha,c_\alpha\), and it invoked Parseval without
an all-mode differentiated Bessel ratio bound in edgewise \(H^{1/2}\).

## New Mechanism

1. Prove the completed support path analytic from the retained exact formulas:
   \(h_\alpha=(\pi/\sum_j\tan(\alpha_j/2))^{1/2}\), the canonical finite-rank
   formula for \(J_\alpha\), and the finite-dimensional analytic Riesz system
   for the exact disk corrector.
2. Construct a second global material map with fixed reference interfaces that
   agrees near each vertex with the physical sector map and rerun the form
   implicit-function theorem for this map.  This proves local annular
   \(C^3(H^1)\) regularity directly; it does not infer it by composing the
   first material eigenfunction.
3. Factor
   \[
   J_\nu(z)=\frac{(z/2)^\nu}{\Gamma(\nu+1)}H_\nu(z^2)
   \]
   on a uniformly small sector.  For \(r\le R/2\), derivatives through order
   three of the ratio \(J_{k\beta}(\sqrt\lambda r)/
   J_{k\beta}(\sqrt\lambda R)\) are a polynomial in \(k\) and
   \(|\log(r/R)|\) times \((r/R)^{k\beta}\).  The exponential factor makes the
   \(k\ge2\) conormal series summable in \(H^1\), hence in \(H^{1/2}\).
   Treat the \(k=1\) Bessel remainder after its leading power separately.

## Reduction Witness

The new material-map IFT and factored Bessel ratio directly close the two
localized audit gaps.  They preserve the frozen statement, all quantifiers,
the scalar/weak flux convention, and the route.  No new hypothesis or uniform
estimate is introduced.
